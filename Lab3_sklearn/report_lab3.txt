InternetService, OnlineSecurity, Contract and PaymentMethod are among the magnitudes with most variability between the customers who churned or stayed.
See feature_distributions_telco.png
I would recommend the company to try to close contracts for as long as possible:
    - 2 year contracts have very low churn
    - Customers on a month-to-month contracts tend to churn often
And also to sell additional products to the same customer:
    - Customers with online security churn a lot less than those without it.
Avoid charging customers via electronic checks
    - It has a much higher churn chance than all other payment methods considered.

The accuracy is always very high (for example, with k=5, the accuracy is 0.7644).
This is not representative because the customers that didn't churn are around 4 times more than those who did.
The dataset is therefore unbalanced and it is easy to increase accuracy by predicting correctly that customers did not leave.

I believe that this imbalance is the biggest limitation of the model.

Approach:
    - Conversion of all cathegorical columns into binary
    - Remove any columns with 0-variance (constant values)
    - Remove columns that are highly correlated
    - Test several K-values
    - Choose the K-value with the highest recall

The most interesting metric for this case is the recall.
We need to minimize the number of false negatives (customers that left the company but were not detected).
